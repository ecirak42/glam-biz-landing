Why this angle

Your wedding look has to survive a camera.

This brand goes after the scarier, more specific fear: not that you will look bad, but that the look you picked will not translate in your ceremony light, close-ups, flash photos, dress neckline, or final gallery.

Risk 01

The makeup reads heavier than you meant.

The audit gives guardrails for finish, contrast, lashes, liner, cheeks, and lips so "soft glam" stays recognizable.

Risk 02

Your colors fight the lighting.

Some tones look beautiful in a palette but pull dull, icy, orange, or harsh near your face and dress.

Risk 03

The hair shape collapses on camera.

Volume, parting, face-framing pieces, and accessory placement matter more once the look is flattened into photos.
