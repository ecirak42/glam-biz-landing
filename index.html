The bride's dilemma

Inspiration is easy. Direction is harder.

Saved looks can show what you like, but they rarely answer the question that matters before the trial: what will actually flatter me?

i

The inspiration gap

Every look you save is beautiful on someone else. None of them explain how it will translate to your face, coloring, hair, dress, or venue light.

ii

The trial should have a starting point

Hair and makeup trials go better when you arrive with language, priorities, and guardrails instead of asking your artist to interpret a folder of mixed references.

iii

Your beauty team needs a clearer brief

“Natural but glam” can mean a dozen different things. A clear plan helps your MUA and hairstylist understand the version that fits you.

iv

You still want to look like you

The goal is not a transformation you barely recognize. It is a polished wedding-day version of your own features, coloring, and personal style.
